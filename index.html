Midnight DUST Inspector — Help Guide

This guide explains every state the Inspector can show, what it means, and what action — if any — you should take. The Inspector is read-only and non-custodial. It never has access to your seed phrase or private keys.

Duplicate / Multiple Registrations

Action needed
⚠ Multiple registrations detected for this stake address.

What it means: The Midnight indexer has found more than one active DUST registration for your stake key. This typically happens when NIGHT arrives in an already-registered wallet, causing the protocol to create a second entry automatically.

What to do

  1. 1Connect your Cardano wallet in the Inspector.
  2. 2Click Deregister to remove the current on-chain registration.
  3. 3Wait for the deregistration to confirm (a few minutes).
  4. 4Click Register now once the state shows "Not registered".

The Timeline section at the bottom of the Inspector shows a full history of all registrations and deregistrations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this tool safe to use? ▼

Yes. The Inspector only reads public blockchain data. It never asks for your seed phrase or private keys. Source code is open and auditable on GitHub.

Why does the Inspector show different data than my Midnight wallet? ▼

The Midnight indexer can lag behind the Cardano blockchain by minutes to hours. The Inspector shows both: the indexer's current view and the actual on-chain state, so you can spot any discrepancy.

My locked vesting NIGHT does not count toward DUST — is that correct? ▼

Yes. Locked vesting NIGHT sits at a script address and is excluded from the DUST cap. Only NIGHT in your regular wallet counts. After claiming, the unlocked NIGHT will be included.

I sent NIGHT to my wallet and now see a duplicate registration. What happened? ▼

Receiving NIGHT into an already-registered wallet can trigger an automatic re-registration, resulting in two entries. De-register once and re-register to resolve it. See Duplicate Registration above.

Can I check someone else's stake address? ▼

Yes. All data shown is public. Enter any stake1… address. You can view their state but cannot register or deregister on their behalf.
