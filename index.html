I just registered or deregistered and nothing has changed ▼

This is normal. The Midnight indexer lags behind the Cardano chain by minutes to several hours. Your transaction is already on-chain and will appear in the Inspector once the indexer catches up.

I see a warning about multiple registrations ▼

This happens when NIGHT arrives in an already-registered wallet — the protocol automatically creates a second registration entry. The Inspector can clean up all extra registrations at once:

  1. 1. Connect both your Cardano wallet and your Midnight wallet, then click Deregister.
  2. 2. The Inspector scans the Cardano contract directly and lists every active registration for your wallet, each with its DUST address. The one matching your connected Midnight wallet is marked ✓ keep; the duplicates are pre-selected for removal.
  3. 3. Review the selection and confirm. All selected registrations are removed in a single transaction, so your wallet asks you to sign just once.
  4. 4. Wait for on-chain confirmation (~5 minutes). If you removed all registrations and the Inspector shows "Not registered", click Register now to register once.

The Inspector reads the live on-chain state (not the slower Midnight indexer), so it always knows how many registrations are really left, and verifies each one belongs to your wallet before removing it.

Midnight

What is Midnight?
Midnight is a data-protection blockchain built alongside Cardano. It uses zero-knowledge proofs to keep transactions private. It has its own network, its own tokens, and its own wallets — but it relies on Cardano for the DUST generation registration mechanism.
What is DUST?
DUST is the native utility token of Midnight, used to pay for transactions on the network. It accumulates automatically in your Midnight wallet once you register a Cardano stake key that holds NIGHT. You do not need to manually claim it — it builds up on its own.
What is the Midnight indexer?
The Midnight indexer reads Cardano chain data and reports registration states to the Midnight network. The Inspector queries this indexer to show your current status. Because it processes blocks after the fact, it can lag behind the actual Cardano chain by minutes to hours. The Inspector also reads the Cardano chain directly via Koios so you can see both views side by side.
What is a DUST address?
Your DUST address is where generated DUST is sent on the Midnight network. It belongs to your Midnight wallet. When you register, you link your Cardano stake key to this address. If you connect a Midnight wallet to the Inspector, it verifies that the registered DUST address matches your connected wallet — a mismatch means DUST may be going elsewhere.

Cardano

What is a stake address?
A stake address (starting with stake1…) represents your staking identity on Cardano. All payment addresses in the same wallet share one stake address. NIGHT held across all those payment addresses is summed together to determine your DUST generation rate and cap. You can find your stake address in your Cardano wallet under Receive, Account, or Staking.
What is NIGHT?
NIGHT is a token that lives on Cardano and powers DUST generation: the more unlocked NIGHT under your registered stake key, the faster DUST accumulates and the higher your cap. Locked NIGHT — held in the vesting contract — does not count toward the cap until you claim it into your regular wallet.
What is registration?
Registration is an on-chain Cardano transaction that links your stake key to a Midnight DUST address. Once registered, the Midnight network knows where to send your DUST. You only need one active registration. Multiple active registrations from the same stake key cause conflicts — the Inspector warns you if it detects this.
What is the registration Timeline?
The Timeline at the bottom of the Inspector shows every registration and deregistration transaction for your stake key, pulled directly from the Cardano chain via Koios. It is independent of the Midnight indexer and always reflects the current on-chain state.

Wallets

The Inspector connects via CIP-30, the standard Cardano dApp connector.

Eternl

· recommended

Full CIP-30 support. Reliable for both reading and signing. Available as a browser extension (Chrome, Firefox, Brave) and as a mobile app. Keep it updated to the latest version.

If the connect button does not respond, try disabling other wallet extensions — multiple wallet extensions can conflict with each other.

Lace

· works fine

IOG's official Cardano wallet. Fully tested with this Inspector. Make sure Lace is unlocked before clicking Connect. Keep it updated.

Nami

· deprecated

Nami is no longer actively maintained. It may still connect but no longer receives security updates. Migrate your funds to Eternl or Lace.

Vespr

· should work

Mobile-first wallet with a desktop browser extension. CIP-30 is supported. Not as widely tested with this Inspector as Eternl or Lace.

Troubleshooting

Wallet not detected
Reload the page. The Inspector checks for wallet extensions on load — installing one after the page opened requires a reload.
Multiple wallets installed
Disable all but one in your browser extension settings to avoid conflicts.
Transaction rejected
Make sure your wallet holds at least 2–3 ADA to cover fees. Registration transactions are cheap but not free.
Wrong network
Ensure your wallet is set to Cardano Mainnet, not a testnet.
Signing popup does not appear
Check whether your browser is blocking popups for this site and allow them.

FAQ

Is this tool safe? Can it access my funds? ▼

No. The Inspector only reads public blockchain data. It never asks for your seed phrase or private keys, and all signing happens inside your wallet extension. When you inspect a stake address, that public address is sent to the configured Midnight indexer and Cardano Koios endpoints so the app can fetch status and timeline data. You can inspect any stake address without connecting a wallet at all. The source code is fully open on GitHub.

Can I check someone else's stake address? ▼

Yes. All data shown is public on-chain information. Enter any stake1… address to inspect it. You can only register or deregister for an address that belongs to a wallet you have connected.

The Inspector shows different data than my Midnight wallet. Why? ▼

The Midnight indexer lags behind the Cardano chain. The Inspector shows both sources separately — the Cardano Timeline is always current and comes directly from on-chain data via Koios, independent of the indexer.

Do I need a Midnight wallet to use this tool? ▼

No. Entering a stake address shows everything without any wallet. Connecting a Cardano wallet adds the ability to register and deregister. Connecting a Midnight wallet adds a live DUST balance and lets the Inspector verify your registered DUST address.

Why does receiving NIGHT create a duplicate registration? ▼

Known protocol behaviour. When new NIGHT arrives in an already-registered wallet, the protocol may automatically create a second registration. The Deregister flow lists every active registration and lets you remove the duplicates in one pass while keeping the one that matches your wallet — see the multiple registrations section above.
