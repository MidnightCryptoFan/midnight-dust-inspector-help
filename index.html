🌙 The Midnight world

What is Midnight?

Midnight is a data-protection blockchain built alongside Cardano. It uses zero-knowledge proofs to let you transact privately. It has its own wallet, its own tokens, and its own network — but it is linked to Cardano for its registration mechanism.

What is DUST?

DUST is the native utility token of the Midnight network. It is generated automatically once you register your NIGHT-holding Cardano stake key. DUST accumulates in your Midnight wallet over time — you do not need to manually trigger or claim it.

DUST is used to pay for transactions on the Midnight network.

What is the Midnight indexer?

The Midnight indexer is a service that reads Cardano blockchain data and reports registration states to the Midnight network. The Inspector queries this indexer to check your current status.

The indexer can lag behind the Cardano chain by minutes to several hours. This is why the Inspector also cross-references the actual on-chain Cardano state via Koios — so you can see both views at once.

What is a DUST address?

Your DUST address is the address on the Midnight network where your generated DUST is sent. It is tied to your Midnight wallet — not your Cardano wallet. When you register, you link your Cardano stake key to a specific DUST address.

If you connect your Midnight wallet to the Inspector, it shows whether your registered DUST address matches your connected Midnight wallet. A mismatch means DUST may be going to a different Midnight address than expected.

🔵 The Cardano world

What is a stake address?

A Cardano stake address (starts with stake1…) represents your staking identity. All payment addresses in the same wallet share the same stake address. NIGHT held across all those payment addresses is summed together for DUST generation.

You can find your stake address in any Cardano wallet under Receive, Account, or Staking details.

What is NIGHT?

NIGHT is Midnight's token that lives on the Cardano blockchain. It is the basis for DUST generation: the more unlocked NIGHT under your registered stake key, the faster DUST accumulates and the higher your DUST cap.

Locked NIGHT — held in a vesting contract at a script address — does not count toward the cap until you claim it into your regular wallet.

What is registration?

Registration is an on-chain Cardano transaction that links your stake key (holding NIGHT) to a Midnight DUST address. Once registered, the Midnight network knows where to send your DUST.

You only need one active registration. Multiple registrations from the same stake key can cause issues — the Inspector will warn you if it detects this.

What is the registration Timeline?

The Timeline section at the bottom of the Inspector shows a chronological history of all registration and deregistration transactions for your stake key, pulled directly from the Cardano blockchain via Koios. This is independent of the Midnight indexer and is always up to date.

👛 Wallet compatibility

The Inspector uses CIP-30 (the Cardano dApp connector standard) to connect to your Cardano wallet. Here is what to expect from each wallet.

Eternl

Recommended

The most feature-complete Cardano wallet. Full CIP-30 support, reliable signing, and works well with this Inspector. Available as a browser extension (Chrome, Firefox, Brave) and as a mobile app.

Minimum version: 2.0 or higher. Always keep Eternl updated.

Known issues: None relevant to this tool.

Tip: If the connect button does not respond, disable other wallet extensions temporarily — multiple wallet extensions can conflict.

Lace

Works, some caveats

IOG's official Cardano wallet. Generally works for connecting and checking your balance, but has had occasional issues with signing transactions in some dApps.

Minimum version: 1.10 or higher recommended.

Known issues: If the Register or Deregister transaction fails or hangs, switch to Eternl and try again.

Tip: Make sure Lace is unlocked before clicking Connect in the Inspector.

Nami

Deprecated — migrate

Nami is no longer actively maintained. It may still connect but is not recommended for new transactions. Migrate your funds to Eternl or Lace.

Known issues: May fail to sign transactions on newer contract versions. No longer receives security updates.

Vespr

Should work

Mobile-first wallet with a desktop browser extension. CIP-30 is supported. Not as widely tested with this Inspector as Eternl.

Tip: If you run into issues, try Eternl as a fallback.

General wallet troubleshooting

  • ·Wallet not detected: Reload the page. The Inspector checks for wallet extensions on load — if you installed one after opening the page, a reload is needed.
  • ·Multiple wallets installed: Only one should be active. Disable the others in your browser extension settings to avoid conflicts.
  • ·Transaction rejected: Make sure you have at least 2–3 ADA in your wallet to cover transaction fees. Registration transactions are cheap but not free.
  • ·Wrong network: Ensure your wallet is set to Cardano Mainnet, not Preprod or Preview testnet.
  • ·Signing popup does not appear: Check if your browser blocked the popup. Allow popups for midnight-dust-inspector.vercel.app.

❓ FAQ

Is this tool safe? Can it access my funds? ▼

No. The Inspector only reads public blockchain data. It never requests your seed phrase or private keys. The wallet connection is only used to read your public key and submit transactions you explicitly sign. Source code is fully open on GitHub.

Can I check someone else's stake address? ▼

Yes. All data shown is public on-chain information. Enter any stake1… address to check its state. You cannot register or deregister on someone else's behalf without connecting their wallet.

The Inspector shows different data than my Midnight wallet. Why? ▼

The Midnight indexer can lag behind the Cardano chain by minutes to hours. The Inspector shows both the indexer view and the actual on-chain state via Koios independently — the Cardano timeline is always current.

Do I need a Midnight wallet to use this tool? ▼

No. You can check any stake address by just typing it in — no wallet connection needed. Connecting a Cardano wallet adds the ability to register and deregister. Connecting a Midnight wallet adds a live DUST balance display and lets the Inspector verify your DUST address.

Why does receiving NIGHT create a duplicate registration? ▼

This is a known protocol behaviour. When new NIGHT arrives in an already-registered wallet, the Midnight protocol may auto-create a second registration entry. De-register and re-register once to resolve it. See the duplicate registration section above.
